Real

The world is full of death, war, decay,
And so we ask, why must it be this way?
We watch as our hearts feel dismay,
And slowly, our ideals, we often may betray.

However, I say, these things they are not real.
And Apple is red, I can touch taste and feel.

Beware that I ask what an Apple is not.
For negative space is infinite, a thought laden with distraught.
An Apple is not a smile, nor a hot cross bun.
Not love, the sky, a song, the moon or the sun.

As such, death is not real, but the absence of life.
With expectation out of the way, we can free our hearts of strife.
And life is real, indeed the greatest gift and joy.
And with time, with this positive shift, something we can enjoy.
